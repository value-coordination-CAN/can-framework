## Integration Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Public  
**Last updated:** 2026-02

---

## Purpose

This document defines principles, requirements, and governance for integrating
external platforms with CAN.

Integrations are treated as governance infrastructure, not mere connectors.

---

## Core Principles

- Purpose limitation
- Proportionality
- Human oversight
- Transparency
- Accountability
- Non-coercion
- Reversibility

---

## Permitted Categories

- Identity & Authentication
- Payments & Settlement (CBDC)
- Simulation & Modelling
- Civic Platforms
- Research & Audit

---

## Prohibited Practices

- Universal scoring
- Covert profiling
- Automated exclusion without appeal
- Surveillance integrations
- Function creep

No integration may create a social credit system.

---

## Lifecycle

1. Proposal
2. Review
3. Pilot
4. Authorisation
5. Monitoring
6. Termination

---

## Rights & Safeguards

- Right to explanation
- Right to appeal
- Right to correction
- Right to human review
- Right to withdrawal

---

## Governance

Each integration must define:
- Responsible authority
- Technical operator
- Oversight body
- Public contact point

---

## Statement of Intent

Technical interoperability must never override social legitimacy.
